Coaching Corner | Do You Really Need a Career Coach? by Moira Farnsworth

January 18, 2016 By acacia Tagged with: Career Coaching, Coaching corner

Yes, I realize, this is a little funny coming from a career coach. And no, it’s not an advertisement.

I receive daily inquiries from professionals about working with me. They come from all types of people – from recent college graduates to experienced professionals looking for their last job before retirement.

Not all of these folks really do “need” me and truthfully, some are better candidates than others for coaching.

So what do I look for in a possible coaching client?

First, time. If someone has very little time to devote to meeting with me or doing follow up work, I will ask that he/she reach back when there is more time available. Career coaching is work, and not just for me. Those being coached need time to take action on assigned tasks.

Secondly, awareness. If someone asks me how quickly they can get a new job making 2x current salary, I will also pass. A great client is one who has a realistic goal – for instance, preparing themselves for a promotion or wanting to explore a new industry. If you’re looking for a get-rich-quick scheme, there are lots of real estate seminars advertised on late night TV. Try those.

Lastly, perseverance. Change doesn’t happen overnight. Career coaching, in its simplest form, is change. REAL change takes time. I’m looking to work with folks who have the fortitude to take a hard look and then do the hard work.
